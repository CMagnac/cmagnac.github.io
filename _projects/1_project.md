## 1. Overview

I sat up an online presence through a personal website on September 2022.
The idea of this project arose in August 2022 after a one year break from coding.
I had basics knowledge on programming and it took me a month writing down and reading documentation to achieve this goal. Then, I choosed Jekyll and GitHub.
Over four months I have deepened my understanding on various aspects of static web development.
You can find the [source code](https://github.com/CMagnac/CMagnac-first-blog) on my GitHub account.

## 2. Deadlines

Reading and taking notes about a subject is irrelevant without pratical applications.
I broke the problem into objectives.
Firstly, I read and practiced with tutorials and the official documentations. I pushed the code on GitHub at the end of September.
Secondly, I wrote the content of my post along with the administration.
Finally, the most difficult part, I accepted the final version as the best result I could provide.

## 3. Completion

I spent a huge amount of time, but the result was rewarding.

I have progressed in five **skills**:

* Manipulating Jekyll, liquid, css, yaml, markedown and the minima theme.
* Applying user experience basics concepts on color and contrast.
* Writing about health and coding subjects.
* Improving indirectly my python skills.
* Organizing my ideas.

I learnt five **principles**:

* Choose images, colors, fonts and fontsize before building.
* Don't create a functionality if you don't need it.
* Being consistant is the key to sucess.
* Always read the terms of service.
* Stay simple and sober.

I did five **mistakes**:

* Even if I have increased my english writing style, I should have written in french.
* I didn't plan enough and wasting so much times on insignificant concepts.
* I took too much time trying to write blog posts with perfection.
* I did to many tasks in a day, that's really a mess.
* I put to much pressure on me.

## 5. Technologies and tools

* [The GitHub plateform](https://docs.github.com/fr) is not only an internet hosting service but also a gold mine of learning ressources.
* [OpenclassRoom video tutorial on GitHub](https://openclassrooms.com/fr/courses/7162856-gerez-du-code-avec-git-et-github), the best french online courses, not so simple but pragmatic.
* [Jekyll official Website](https://jekyllrb.com/) is a static site generator, a good way to express creativity and share ideas.
* [Mike Dane youtube tutorials](https://www.youtube.com/@GiraffeAcademy/featured) are the best video tutorial to learn Jekyll, clear and effective.
* [Freepik](https://www.freepik.com/), my favourite images provider. The choice of their free vector images is striking.
* [A contrast color analyze](https://www.tpgi.com/color-contrast-checker/) is a usefull tool to provide a good colour visibility.
* [Compressing your images](https://compressjpeg.com/) is one of the way to increase  site speed.
* [I use a plagiat checker](https://smallseotools.com/fr/plagiarism-checker/) to avoid any redundancy on my blog posts.
* Center in css is a real pain that's why I use this [online tool](http://howtocenterincss.com/).
