Hypnosis has been used for over a century for various purposes, including non-conventional therapeutic medicine, relaxation, and psychotherapy goals. Although we don't fully understand how hypnosis works, new technologies like virtual reality have allowed us to explore different applications.

If you're interested in trying hypnosis without virtual reality, there are many practitioners available. However, it's important to be aware of the existence of charlatans or quacks.

The **National Institute of Health** (NIH) provides a good definition of hypnosis.

<blockquote>
A trance-like state in which a person becomes more aware and focused on particular thoughts, feelings, images, sensations, or behaviors. While under hypnosis, a person may feel calm, relaxed, and more open to suggestion. Hypnosis is usually done with the help of a specially trained therapist. It may be used to help relieve stress, anxiety, and pain, and to help a person quit smoking or lose weight. Hypnosis is a type of complementary and alternative medicine (CAM).
</blockquote>

## About hypnosis

The field of hypnosis utilization includes:

* Hypnosedation (anesthesia)
* Hypnoanalgesia (pain management)
* Hypnotherapy (psychotherapeutic use)

While there are still many unanswered questions regarding how hypnosis produces its beneficial effects, **three factors** play an important role: biological, psychological, and social factors.

Factors like structural connectivity, hemisphere asymmetry, higher levels of theta bandwidth activity, expectancies, trait hypnotizability, motivation, absorptive capacity, rapport, and context can facilitate the hypnotic response.

On one hand, psychiatrists, psychotherapists, or hypnotherapists may be good choices for trying hypnosis.

On the other hand, it's important to note that hypnosis is considered a type of complementary and alternative medicine, and some **charlatans** take advantage of vulnerable individuals.

With the advancements in technology, new therapeutic approaches use **virtual reality** (VR) to induce hypnosis for medical purposes.

The official definition provided by the **Haute Autorité de Santé** (HAS), the French High Authority of Health, outlines the application domains of VR, which include :

* training of health professionals
* relaxation and stress reduction
* psychology and psychiatry
* pain relief
* re-education
* treatment of neurodegenerative diseases
* anesthesia, and surgery

<blockquote>
The use of equipment (such as a dummy or a procedural simulator), virtual reality or a standardized patient, to reproduce care situations or environments, to teach diagnostic and therapeutic procedures and to allow processes, clinical situations or decision-making by a healthcare professional or a team of professionals.
</blockquote>

Virtual reality can also have a **class 1 medical device status**, as defined by the **French Public Health Code** and European texts.

<blockquote>
An instrument, device or equipment intended for use in humans for purposes, in particular, of diagnosis, prevention, control of disease or injury. The main action of a medical device is not obtained by pharmacological or immunological means or by metabolism, it is essentially mechanical. Software intended to be used specifically for diagnostic or therapeutic purposes also constitutes a medical device.
</blockquote>

## Virtual Reality Companies

For the past 30 years, start-ups and companies have been working on using VR to induce hypnosis, a field of research known as **Virtual Reality Exposure Therapies** (VRET) or *Thérapies par Exposition à la Réalité Virtuelle* (TERV) in French.

The application domains of VRET/TERV include anesthesia and intensive care, surgery, dental care, imaging, oncology, immersive therapy, anxiety, and pain management.

While I have mentioned five companies, there are many more around the world.

### 1. HypnoVR

Website : [Hypnovr](https://hypnovr.io/en/).
Founded in 2016.
Located in **Strasbourg**, France.
Provides medical hypnosis to 240 health facilities.
Their goal is to reduce the usage of anxiolytics and sedatives for the treatment of stress, pain, and anxiety.
It's a **class 1 medical device**.

### 2. Healthymind

Website : [healtymind](https://healthymind.fr/).
Founded in 2017.
Located in **Strasbourg**, France.
Primed by the University of Adelaide (Australia).
Offers a contemplative and interactive device.
Their goal is to address anxiety and pain.

### 3. Lumeen

Website : [lumeen](https://www.lumeen.com/).
Founded in 2019.
Specializes in the most widespread type of French residential care for senior citizens, *les Etablissement d'Hébergement pour Personnes Agées Dépendantes* (EHPAD).
Gained class 1 medical device status in April 2021.
Provides services to 400 health facilities.

### 4. C2Care

Website : [C2Care](https://www.c2.care/fr/).
Founded in 2015.
Located in Sanary-Sur-Mer, France.
They collaborate with 22 University Hospital Centers.
They provide many applications : C2Phobia, C2Addict, C2Drive(fear of driving), C2Hypno (meditation), Physio (physiotherapy), Nitro (eating disorder), Neuro (support for brain-damaged people), Companion (stimulation of cognitive function), Brain (cognitive) and Custom (custom software).

### 5. Amelia Virtual Care

Website : [amelia virtual care](https://ameliavirtualcare.com/)
Founded in 2013.
Located in Barcelona.

About amelia virtual care :

*it has developed the largest and most complete virtual reality platform in mental health, allowing professionals to treat a wide range of mental health disorders from common phobias to anxiety and depression.*
