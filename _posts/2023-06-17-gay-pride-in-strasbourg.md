## Informations on HIV

HIV type 1 and HIV type 2 (HIV-1, HIV-2) are the agents of Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome (AIDS). HIV-1 was discovered in 1983 among male homosexuals affected by this new disease.

According to Joint United Nations Programme on HIV and AIDS ([UNAIDS](https://www.unaids.org/en)) estimates from 2012, there are 35 million infected individuals, mainly in Africa and Asia. The number of new cases is estimated at 2.3 million per year. In France, the number of new infections has remained steady at around 6,500 cases per year since 2003, with approximately 150,000 infected individuals.

The phylogenetic groups M and N of HIV-1 correspond to two independent interspecies transmissions of **Simian Immunodeficiency Virus** (SIV) from a subspecies of chimpanzee, *Pan troglodytes troglodytes*. Groups O and P originate from gorilla SIV. The first zoonotic transmissions from chimpanzees probably occurred between 1900 and 1920.

Initially, SIV is non-pathogenic, but infection in humans and certain monkey species leads to AIDS.

I will focus on PrEP, which stands for **Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis**. The brand name of the drug commonly used for PrEP is TRUVADA&reg;, which comes in 200 mg/245 mg tablets. The international non-proprietary names (INN) for the active ingredients in TRUVADA are emtricitabine (200 mg) and tenofovir disoproxil (245 mg).

You can find more informations about PrEP in french [here](https://www.aides.org/prep).

## Focus on PrEP

It's a drug to be taken in the goal to prevent HIV in Men Who Have Sex with Men (MSM).

You can find more informations about MSM on [Centers for Disease Control and  Prevention](https://www.cdc.gov/std/treatment-guidelines/msm.htm).

The PrEP is prescribed only to HIV-negative people over 18 who regularly take HIV risks.

* Men who have sex with men.
* Sex workers and their partners.
* People who come from countries where there are many people infected with HIV and their partners.
* Partners of people living with HIV who have not reached an undetectable viral load.
* People who use drugs by injection (with syringe exchange).

There are two main dose administrations.

* Continuous PrEP: this involves taking 1 pill every day at the same time for a certain period of time.
* PrEP on demand (only for men): it involves taking two tablets minimum 2 hours and maximum 24 hours before sexual intercourse and then taking one tablet the next day at the same time and a second 24 hours later.

PrEP can only be prescribed by a doctor affiliated with an HIV Reference Center after a medical consultation. If the person meets the reimbursement criteria, in France PrEP is eligible for reimbursement.

The primary issue is that PrEP does not provide protection against other sexually transmitted diseases. It goes without saying that the best and most cost-effective option is to use a condom.

Below is a list of the main sexually transmitted diseases for which there are no preventive drugs available, and the only preventive measure is the use of condoms.

* Calymmatobacterium granulomatis
* Chancroid
* Gonorrhea
* Syphilis
* Trichomonosis

As you have correctly noted, chemsex refers to the combination of chemical substances with sexual activity. The objective is to enhance sexual desire or prolong sexual encounters using drugs.

Stimulating and euphoric drugs are commonly chosen for this purpose, including cathinones (such as 4-MEC, 3-MMC, etc.), cocaine, methamphetamine, and ecstasy/MDMA. Ketamine and GHB are also occasionally used. Additionally, individuals engaging in chemsex may use drugs that promote erection, consume alcohol, and use poppers.

The practice of using drugs to enhance sexual experiences is not a new concept, but the detrimental effects on one's health are well-known.

### Risks

The risks associated with using these drugs are important. If you use these substances, you may:

* Face a high risk of addiction and, in some cases, fatal overdoses.
* Encounter significant risks of developing abscesses, wounds, and other physical complications.
* Have an increased likelihood of acquiring sexually transmitted infections (STIs) and being infected with HIV or hepatitis C.
* Develop a desire to inject the drugs ("slam") in pursuit of stronger sensations.
* Be at risk of experiencing relationship breakdowns, losing friends, jeopardizing your job, and finding yourself isolated and in a state of severe distress.

It is crucial to be aware of all these risks. If you find yourself in any of these situations, it is advisable to seek guidance from a **healthcare professional**.
